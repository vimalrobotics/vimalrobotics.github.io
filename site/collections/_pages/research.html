Published Research

Stormbreaker: An Assistive Autonomous Towing Robot
IEEE Conference Publication, 2023

This research presents the design and implementation of an assistive autonomous robotic system capable of towing a wheelchair in real-world environments. The system integrates ROS-based navigation, sensor fusion, and safety-aware control strategies to enable reliable autonomous motion alongside human users.

Research Interests

  • Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs)
  • ROS 2-based robotics software architectures
  • Sensor fusion and perception-driven control
  • Assistive and human-centered robotics
  • Embedded systems and edge intelligence

Current Direction

My current focus lies in bridging academic robotics research with deployable real-world systems, emphasizing robustness, safety, and maintainability in autonomous platforms.
